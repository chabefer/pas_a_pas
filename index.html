English

Hello everyone and welcome to my «Pas-à-Pas» webpage.

I have named my website «Pas-à-Pas» (French for Step by Step) because I believe that it is only by slowly accumulating rigorous empirical evidence that we are going to tackle the social problems of our time. I am trying to set «Pas-à-Pas» as a stepping stone in that process. With «Pas-à-Pas», I would like to do slightly more than present my research and teaching. I would like to create an interface for everyone to use in order to learn about what I am doing but also about the more general context of my research areas. On top of my colleagues knowing what I’m up to, I would like «Pas-à-Pas» to be a resource for students, citizens, journalists and policymakers in search for up-to-date evidence-based information on my main research areas.

In the “Econometrics of Causal Inference” section of «Pas-à-Pas», I am going to progressively upload, on top of my research results, the full content of my “Econometrics of Program Evaluation” class. I have designed this class so as to be self-contained and accompanied by the corresponding R code. My hope with making this material widely accessible is that it will enable more students to learn and understand key empirical tools of causal inference.

In the “Agro-Environmental Policies” section of «Pas-à-Pas», I plan to add, on top of my own research, sections about the literature giving a broader view of the main issues and results. I might also decide to upload there the content of my “Empirical Environmental Economics” class.

In the “Education” section of «Pas-à-Pas», I mainly want to summarize in an accessible manner the available empirical evidence on the most effective educational programs trying to improve the lives of children from destitute backgrounds. As I would like to communicate this information widely in France, this last section will mainly be in French.

In the “Teaching Economics as Science” section of «Pas-à-Pas», I will present examples of major empirical results in economics and the way that I think they should be included in the undergraduate economics curriculum.

Please, do not refrain from commenting on any section of the website. It will only make it better.
